SAVAN IS A SPECIAL MONDAY

SAVAN IS THE MOST SPECIAL MONTH OF LORD SHIV

SAVAN IS THE MOST BELOVED MONTH OF LORD SHIV>

During the month of Sawan, devotees of Lord Shiva observe fasts and offer prayers to seek his blessings. The month is considered highly auspicious for worshiping Lord Shiva, and many people visit temples to perform rituals and participate in religious ceremonies.

Devotees often visit Shiva temples, especially on Mondays, which are considered particularly sacred for Lord Shiva. They offer milk, water, and Bilva leaves to the Shiva Lingam, chant mantras, and recite prayers. The month of Sawan is also associated with various festivals and fairs dedicated to Lord Shiva, attracting large gatherings of devotees.

Overall, Sawan is a time of devotion, spiritual reflection, and seeking the blessings of Lord Shiva for health, prosperity, and well-being.

RITUALS OF SAVAN

 starting from the first day of Sawan, devotees observe fasts and perform special prayers dedicated to Lord Shiva. They visit temples, offer milk, water, and Bilva leaves to the Shiva Lingam, and chant mantras. Many people also participate in religious processions and cultural events organized during this month. The rituals may vary based on regional traditions, but the essence of devotion and reverence for Lord Shiva remains central to the observances of Sawan.
